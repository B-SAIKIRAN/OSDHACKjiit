FAQs

What's a Hackathon, and can I participate?
expand_more
A Hackathon is an intense event that brings together designers and developers along with industry experts to identify problems and create software solutions, usually within 24-48 hours. Whether that's high school, undergrad, or graduate, we believe you can get something out of the event. Only students of JIIT are allowed to participate in the offline OSDHack.
Can participants use the equipment provided by the organizers in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants must bring their own laptops and other necessary equipment. Internet connection will be provided by the organizers via RJ-45 cables.
NOTE: MacBook users need to bring their own port-extenders.
Is registration required to participate in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Yes, participants are required to register for the event prior to the deadline.
Can participants make teams with members from outside the college in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants are not allowed to make teams with members from outside the college. However JIIT-62 and JIIT-128 participants can make teams together.
How many members can be in a team in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Each team should consist of at least 4 and at most 6 members. Pro Tip: Aim to have a mix of people with both design and developer skills.
What if I don't have a team or an idea?
expand_more
Don't worry, most people don’t! We’ll have team formation and ideation events geared towards helping you find people to work with. It’s perfectly normal to go around to people, tell them you’re looking for a team, and ask if you can help out. You can also show yourself off at #looking-for-teammates
Can participants use copyrighted materials in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
No, participants must not use any copyrighted materials.
How should participants submit their final project in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Participants must submit their final project within the given time limits.
What happens if a participant violates the rules or engages in misbehavior in the offline hackathon?
expand_more
Any kind of misbehavior or violation of the rules can lead to disqualification from the competition.
Where can participants contact the organizers for more information?
